# Information Analysis Quality Gate

Use this checklist before delivering an analytical answer, note, recommendation, or review.

## Minimum viable analysis

A useful analysis must answer:

```text
What question is being answered?
What is the best current answer?
Why this answer rather than alternatives?
How confident are we and why?
What could change the answer?
What should be checked or done next?
```

If any of these are missing, the output is probably a summary, not an analysis.

## Red flags

### Question failures

- The output answers a topic, not a question.
- The question contains the favored explanation.
- The audience or decision is not named.
- Boundaries are missing, so any information can be added forever.

### Source failures

- Source authority is treated as proof.
- Several dependent sources are counted as independent confirmation.
- Freshness, proximity, incentives, or completeness are ignored.
- A source is used to prove more than it can know.

### Evidence failures

- Facts, interpretations, assumptions, and conclusions are mixed.
- The output lists evidence but does not say what it supports or weakens.
- Contradictions are not discussed.
- Absence of expected signs is ignored.

### Hypothesis failures

- Only one explanation is considered.
- Alternatives repeat the same mechanism with different wording.
- Confirmation is counted mechanically.
- The analysis asks “what supports this?” but not “what would weaken this?”

### Uncertainty failures

- Confidence is unstated.
- Confidence is stated without reason.
- “Need more data” appears without naming exact data and its effect.
- Unknowns are not tied to action or confidence.

### Recommendation failures

- Recommendation is stronger than evidence.
- A full action is recommended when only a reversible test is justified.
- Load-bearing assumptions are hidden.
- No stop condition or revision condition is stated.

### Communication failures

- The answer is buried under process narrative.
- The memo shows effort but no answer.
- Caveats are generic and do not change anything.
- The output does not tell the reader what to do or check next.

## Scoring rubric

Use 0/1/2 scoring.

| Criterion | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Question | absent/topic | partially clear | clear, neutral, bounded |
| Context/use | absent | implied | audience/decision named |
| Sources | unexamined | basic list | quality and limits assessed |
| Claim discipline | mixed | some separation | facts/interpretations/assumptions separated |
| Alternatives | absent | weak | real competing mechanisms |
| Evidence comparison | pile | partial | diagnostic evidence and contradictions named |
| Unknowns | generic | some gaps | exact gaps tied to confidence/action |
| Assumptions | hidden | partly named | load-bearing assumptions checked |
| Confidence | absent/decorative | stated | justified and bounded |
| Recommendation | overstrong/absent | plausible | proportional with next check/revision |

Interpretation:

- **0–8:** not yet analysis.
- **9–14:** usable draft, needs strengthening.
- **15–20:** strong analytical output.

## Output self-test

Before sending, answer in one sentence each:

1. The question is...
2. The current best answer is...
3. The answer rests mainly on...
4. The strongest alternative is...
5. Confidence is not higher because...
6. The next check/action is...
7. I would change my conclusion if...

If you cannot fill these without handwaving, continue analysis.

## Language quality for Russian analytical text

For Russian outputs, avoid:

- `в современном мире`;
- `важный аспект`;
- `играет ключевую роль`;
- `позволяет эффективно`;
- `комплексный процесс`;
- `данный подход`;
- `осуществить анализ`;
- `реализовать проверку`;
- `адресовать проблему`;
- `доставить ценность`;
- `версия про` when `версия о`, `гипотеза о`, or `объяснение через` is better.

Prefer verbs and visible subjects:

- `проверить`, not `осуществить проверку`;
- `по графику нельзя понять причину`, not `график различает причины`;
- `таблица помогает проверить вывод`, not `таблица объясняет вывод`;
- `вывод держится на допущении`, not `данный подход позволяет`.
